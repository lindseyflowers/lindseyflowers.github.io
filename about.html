Lindsey Flowers



Growing

I was born an army brat but raised a Northern Virginia native. Before settling in Alexandria, VA we lived in the southern U.S. and Italy (no, I'm not fluent; yes, the gelato was amazing).

Everytime I look in the mirror I look a little more like my mom and everytime I speak I sound a little more like my dad. I try to encompass the best parts of both of them: my mom's ability to fight through trying times, and my dad's persistency in doing what's right.

I've been very priveled growing up, both with the location and my support system. I'm going to use these rooots to continue growing, becoming a person who fights for what she believes in and does whatever it takes to get there, no matter the obstacles.

Learning

I'm a public relations major and professional writing and social change + leadership minor at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, Virginia. I want to use my degree to do some good in the world, whether it be at a non-profit, community-minded organization or government agency. Public relations combines my love of communication, the written word and fostering meaningful relationships perfectly.

Now that I finally have some direction and have found what I am passionate about, I am working to be able to use my degree to improve other’s lives. My formal schooling may almost be complete, but I'm far from done learning: I'm going to continue learning how to use best serve my community with the skillset my education has provided.

Thriving

I believe in getting the most out of every day—in doing more than merely existing.

My time at Virginia Tech has given me purpose. It was through living here that I discovered my love of hiking, of the outdoors and of dancing like nobody is watching. I've realized that my time is what I make of it, and I want to spend it helping others, being unafraid of new possibilities and embracing my individuality.

Because of it, I no longer feel content to just exist. I may not know exactly what I want to do, but I know I want to make other’s lives better. I push myself to do things that matter, to pick a career where I will thrive, to help others, and be happy with my own existence.
